The Grand River is also a very popular body of water, going throughout Brantford, and connecting it to most of Ontario. In the early 1800s it was used as a source of not only water but transportation and power for local communities. Brantford was, at one point, a shipping port. It is a local hotspot for things like fishing and tubing, depending on the season of course. They would eventually build a dike, a large man-made hill that essentially works as a dam to help prevent flooding to the neighborhoods near the river. There are many trails through the forests and around the river as well that you can explore and enjoy! The city grows more and more each year, bringing in more diversity and future attractions to keep the city alive and thriving.
